Global Cancer Data Visualizations (2017)

Data retrieved from ourworldindata.org/cancer
Region

Prevalence (%) of Cancer per Age Group (2017)

Globally, ~6% of those over 70 had cancer in 2017. Of the population younger than 50 the prevalence is under 1%. The general trend across all regions is that older age groups have more prevalence of cancer, but it does vary slightly depending on region. This could be for various reasons pertaining to quality of life, based on economic, environmental, and other factors.

Region

Prevalence (%) of Cancer Type for Total Population (2017)

Breast cancer is the most common cancer worldwide. The frequency of certain cancer types per region varies based on a multitude of factors. For instance, in areas where air quality is poor, it can be expected to see high occurences of trachael, bronchus, and lung cancer. Stomach cancer is more common in Asian countries, which is believed to be due largely in part to diet.

Region

Total Deaths per Cancer Type (2017)

Unfortunately, cancer is a leading cause of death worldwide, and will probably stay that way until more advances are made in battling it. Trachael, bronchus, and lung cancer, particularly lung, has a very high fatality rate, which could be due to there being many areas with poor air quality, as well as limited treatment options. One would expect that frequency of type would generally reflect higher death rates. Certain types are also just very hard to beat, such as pancreatic cancer.
